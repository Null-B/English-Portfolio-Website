I read this article from the BBC about how social media has slowly changed from a platform where we interacted to a video and entertainment platform where users only go to spend time and watch content.
The article describes a slow but steady change since the dawn of short-form content, where users have stopped interacting with each other and are more focused on watching content. This shift is most apparent on Instagram. A couple of years ago, Instagram was a platform to see what your friends were doing, like each other's posts, and view each other's stories. But that has changed. In modern social media, you rarely see posts from your friends because they are covered by so many other videos.

This shows us that the algorithm has changed its focus from showing you the people in your social network to showing you what you want to see in order to keep you engaged with the app.

"18% active for 74% passive." - BBC
This was the ratio of young Gen Z users and their posting habits. As we can see, the small 18 percent also includes professional content.
This reduction in activity has several causes. People have learned that what you post on the web will stay on the web. That's why people are very cautious about what they share on the internet, and this, in turn, drives many away from posting anything.

User habits have shown that apps like TikTok and Instagram are primarily used for consuming content, and when people want to interact with each other, they have moved to platforms like WhatsApp or Snapchat that are more intimate.
