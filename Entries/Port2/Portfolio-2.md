# Protfolio 2 Walking Home at Night
There are rarely moments in my life when nothing is happening — not working, not talking, not watching, not learning. When there is such a moment of silence, it feels special to me, because I get to reflect on what has happened or what’s yet to come.

This usually happens when I walk home at night, after a night out with friends where things happen one after another. Half the time, someone goes missing only to be found again a few minutes later. During this rush of constant activity, your mind can’t really grasp everything. But once the dust settles, it’s late, and I’m on my way home — that’s when I start realizing things: why my friend disappeared for a while, why that stranger was angry at us, or why those girls looked so surprised.

And then it hits you — ah, that’s why that happened. It’s a funny realization because you notice that, in the chaos, you’ve missed details that would’ve been obvious anywhere else.

It’s strange but peaceful. You finally relax your senses, take in the cool night air, and go home — ready to collapse onto your bed.
